About Me

I am a postdoctoral researcher in the Stochastics Group at Aarhus University. My research sits at the interface of probability theory, random graph models, and stochastic processes. I study spatially embedded networks governed by Gibbsian interactions, with emphasis on large deviation principles, rare events, and limiting behavior.

I also teach courses in probability theory and random complex networks at the graduate level. I completed my PhD at Eindhoven University of Technology (TU/e) within the NETWORKS program, affiliated with the Probability group. My thesis was titled “Centrality measures and connectivity properties in large networks.”

Contact: m.pandey@math.au.dk
