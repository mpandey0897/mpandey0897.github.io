About Me

Hello there, and a warm welcome to my virtual abode! As a postdoctoral researcher in the Stochastics group at Aarhus University, I work on problems at the interface of probability theory, random graph models, and stochastic geometry. My current research explores the behavior of spatially embedded networks governed by Gibbsian interactions, with a focus on understanding their structural and probabilistic properties. A significant part of this work involves the development of large deviation principles for spatial network models and the study of rare events and limiting behavior in such systems.

Alongside my research activities, I am involved in teaching courses in probability theory and random complex networks, contributing to the graduate-level curriculum within the mathematics department. I completed my PhD in the Department of Mathematics and Computer Science at Eindhoven University of Technology (TU/e), Netherlands, as a member of the NETWORKS program, affiliated with the Probability group.
